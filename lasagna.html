Lasagna is one of Italy's most iconic comfort foods, originating from the fertile region of Emilia-Romagna, where culinary traditions run deep. This layered masterpiece combines wide sheets of pasta with a rich, slow-cooked meat ragu made from ground beef or pork, tomatoes, and a hint of red wine. Each layer is carefully assembled with a generous coating of creamy béchamel sauce and a sprinkling of Parmigiano Reggiano, resulting in a deeply satisfying dish. The bubbling layers of sauce and cheese, golden-brown and slightly crisp on top, are a feast for both the eyes and the taste buds.

What makes lasagna truly special is its depth of flavor and texture, a beautiful balance of hearty ragu, silky béchamel, and tender pasta that melts together as it bakes. Each bite reveals a comforting, rich taste, capturing the essence of Italian cooking: passion, patience, and a respect for ingredients. More than just a meal, lasagna is a celebration of Italian heritage and a dish that brings people together, perfect for family gatherings or festive occasions. This beloved classic represents the heart of Italian cuisine, embodying warmth, tradition, and the unmistakable joy of sharing food with loved ones.

Ingredienti

  • 12 lasagna sheets
  • 500g ground beef
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 250g ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 1 large egg
  • 200g mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparazione

  1. Prepare meat sauce by simmering ground beef with tomato sauce, onions, and garlic.
  2. Mix ricotta cheese with grated Parmigiano, an egg, salt, and pepper.
  3. Layer lasagna sheets, meat sauce, ricotta mixture, and mozzarella in a baking dish.
  4. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 30–40 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  5. Let rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
