Spaghetti alla Carbonara is a Roman classic, loved for its creamy, savory flavor and simple yet rich ingredients. Traditionally made with just eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, guanciale (cured pork cheek), and black pepper, Carbonara showcases the elegance of Italian cooking without the need for heavy cream. The magic happens when the eggs and cheese emulsify with the pasta water, forming a silky sauce that clings to each strand of spaghetti. Every bite offers a delicious contrast: the saltiness of the guanciale, the sharpness of the Pecorino, and the warmth of the pepper, capturing the essence of Italian comfort food.

This dish embodies the Roman passion for bold flavors and minimal ingredients, making it a beloved staple in Italian homes. Spaghetti alla Carbonara is a testament to the art of simplicity and precision, transforming a handful of pantry ingredients into a luscious, satisfying meal with layers of taste and texture.

Ingredienti

  • 200g spasta
  • 100g guanciale (or pancetta)
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Preparazione

  1. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water.
  2. Dice and cook guanciale in a pan until crispy; set aside, leaving the fat in the pan.
  3. Whisk egg yolks with grated Pecorino and black pepper until smooth.
  4. Add drained pasta to the pan with guanciale, then remove from heat and mix in the egg mixture, stirring quickly.
  5. Adjust with pasta water for creaminess, garnish with extra Pecorino and pepper, and serve immediately.
