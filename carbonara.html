Carbonara is one of Italy's most beloved and iconic dishes, originating from the Lazio region, particularly Rome. Made with a few simple ingredients—pasta, guanciale (cured pork cheek), eggs, pecorino cheese, and black pepper—this dish is a testament to the beauty of traditional Italian cooking.

The magic of carbonara lies in its rich, creamy sauce, created without the use of cream, but rather from the perfect blend of egg yolks, cheese, and pasta water. This hearty, flavorful pasta embodies the essence of Italian cuisine: simplicity, quality ingredients, and timeless taste.

Ingredienti

  • 200g spasta
  • 100g guanciale (or pancetta)
  • 50g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Preparazione

  1. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water.
  2. Dice and cook guanciale in a pan until crispy; set aside, leaving the fat in the pan.
  3. Whisk egg yolks with grated Pecorino and black pepper until smooth.
  4. Add drained pasta to the pan with guanciale, then remove from heat and mix in the egg mixture, stirring quickly.
  5. Adjust with pasta water for creaminess, garnish with extra Pecorino and pepper, and serve immediately.
