Panna Cotta is a classic Italian dessert that exemplifies elegance through simplicity. Originally from the Piedmont region, this dessert is made by blending cream, sugar, and vanilla, then setting it with a touch of gelatin to create a smooth, delicate texture. Panna cotta is often served with a drizzle of berry coulis, caramel, or chocolate, adding a burst of flavor to its silky, custard-like base.

This refined dessert captures the Italian love for subtle flavors and creamy textures, offering a light yet satisfying end to a meal. With its gentle vanilla notes and luxurious consistency, panna cotta is a dessert thatâ€™s as visually appealing as it is delicious, embodying the Italian philosophy of balance and finesse.

Ingredienti

  • 500ml heavy cream
  • 100ml milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 packet (7g) gelatin powder, softened in water
  • Fresh berries or berry sauce (for serving, optional)

Preparazione

  1. Heat cream, milk, and sugar in a saucepan until sugar dissolves.
  2. Add vanilla extract and softened gelatin, stirring until fully dissolved.
  3. Pour the mixture into molds and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  4. Run a knife around the edges to release panna cotta from molds.
  5. Serve with fresh berries or berry sauce.
