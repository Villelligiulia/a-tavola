Tiramisù is Italy's beloved dessert, born in the Veneto region and cherished for its layers of rich, creamy, and bittersweet flavors. This dessert is crafted with ladyfingers soaked in espresso, layered with a smooth mascarpone cheese mixture, and dusted with a generous amount of cocoa powder. The combination of coffee-soaked biscuits, creamy cheese, and a hint of chocolate creates a luxurious dessert that melts in the mouth.

Tiramisù means "pick-me-up" in Italian, and each spoonful truly lives up to its name, offering a perfect balance of sweetness and bitterness. With its simplicity and elegance, tiramisù remains a timeless classic, enjoyed at gatherings and celebrations as a symbol of Italian indulgence and hospitality.

Ingredienti

  • 200g ladyfingers (savoiardi)
  • 250g mascarpone cheese
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup brewed coffee, cooled
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting

Preparazione

  1. Brew strong coffee and let it cool.
  2. Beat egg yolks with sugar, then fold in mascarpone cheese.
  3. Dip ladyfingers in coffee and layer them in a dish.
  4. Spread mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers and repeat layers.
  5. Refrigerate for 4 hours, dust with cocoa powder before serving.
